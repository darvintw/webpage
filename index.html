SHORT NOTES

PROBABILITY & STATISTICS

  • Sufficient Statistics: Introduces the definition of sufficient statistics and minimal sufficient statistics, Sufficiency Principle and Fisher's thought experiment.
  • Martingale: Introduces the basic definitions of martingale theory, several real life examples, and the convergence of martingale.
  • Three Proofs for CLT: Introduces three different proofs for the Central Limit Theorem: De'Moivre's proof, Lindeberg's proof and Charles Stein's proof.
  • Martingale and Harmonic Functions: Introduces the connection between martingale and harmonic functions. The fact that any nonnegative super-harmonic function defined on R and R^2 must be constant can be proved using martingale theory.
  • Statistical Distances: Introduces several commonly used statistical distances, such as total variance, Hellinger distance and KL divergence.

ECONOMETRICS

  • E[XX'] and E[XZ'] full rank?: In econometrics, one often encounter the assumption that E[XX'] / E[XZ'] is full rank. This note introduces the meaning of such assumptions.
